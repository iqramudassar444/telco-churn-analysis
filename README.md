# Telco Customer Churn Analysis Dashboard

**End-to-End Data Analytics Project | Python + Pandas + Matplotlib + Seaborn**

---

## 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes **customer churn behavior** in a telecom company.
The main goal is to identify **why customers leave the service** and uncover patterns that affect customer retention.

This project demonstrates a complete data analytics workflow — from **data cleaning and preprocessing** to **exploratory data analysis (EDA)** and **dashboard creation using Python**.

### Key areas analyzed:

* Customer churn trends
* Contract and tenure behavior
* Service usage patterns
* Monthly and total charges analysis
* Customer risk segmentation
* Correlation between important features

---

## 🏢 About the Business Context

Telecommunication companies often face customer churn, where customers stop using their services and switch to competitors.

Customer churn directly affects:

* **Revenue**
* **Customer retention**
* **Business growth**
* **Long-term profitability**

This analysis helps understand **which customers are at high risk of leaving** and what factors influence their decision.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem

The objective of this project is to analyze telecom customer data and identify the major factors contributing to churn.

### Key business questions addressed:

* Which customers are more likely to churn?
* How does contract type affect churn?
* Do new customers leave more often?
* Does monthly billing impact churn behavior?
* Which services are linked with higher churn?
* What customer groups are most at risk?

## 🔄 Data Analytics Workflow

This project follows a structured analytics workflow:

1. Raw customer dataset loaded into **Python**
2. Data cleaned and transformed using **Pandas**

4. Exploratory Data Analysis (**EDA**) performed
5. Key churn indicators identified
6. Multiple dashboards created using **Matplotlib** and **Seaborn**

---

## 📊 Performance Overview

### Key findings from the analysis:

* Customers with **month-to-month contracts** show the highest churn
* **New customers (0–6 months tenure)** are more likely to leave
* Customers with **higher monthly charges** tend to churn more
* Some services such as **Fiber Optic** show higher churn behavior
* Customers using **electronic check** payment method have higher churn risk

---

## 🛍 Service & Customer Behavior Analysis

### Insights from customer and service analysis:

* Customers without **Online Security**, **Tech Support**, and **Device Protection** churn more often
* Customers with **short-term contracts** are less loyal
* Customers paying **higher monthly charges** are more likely to leave
* Customers in the early stage of their subscription are the most vulnerable to churn

---

## 🌍 Customer Segmentation Insights

The analysis highlights different churn risk groups:

* **High-risk customers** → Month-to-month + high monthly charges
* **Medium-risk customers** → Mid-tenure + selective service usage
* **Low-risk customers** → Long-term contracts + stable payment behavior

This helps businesses identify **which customer segments need immediate retention action**.

---

## 👥 Customer Retention Analysis

Customer retention patterns revealed important insights:

* The **first few months** are the most critical for customer retention
* Long-term customers are more likely to stay
* Churn is strongly influenced by:

  * Contract type
  * Payment method
  * Service usage
  * Monthly charges

### Business meaning:

This indicates the need for **better onboarding, support, and pricing strategies**.

---

## 📈 Strategic Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following actions are recommended:

* Encourage customers to move from **month-to-month contracts** to **yearly plans**
* Improve onboarding experience for **new customers**
* Offer discounts or bundles for customers with **high monthly charges**
* Promote **automatic payment methods**
* Encourage adoption of support-related services such as:

  * Online Security
  * Tech Support
  * Device Protection
* Build targeted retention campaigns for **high-risk customer groups**
